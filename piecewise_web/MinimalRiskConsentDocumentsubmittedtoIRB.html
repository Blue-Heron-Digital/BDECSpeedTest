Research Participant Information and Consent Form

You are being asked to participate in a research project that will help to improve broadband access in Michigan and the United States. Researchers are required to provide a consent form to inform you about the research study, to convey that participation is voluntary, to explain risks and benefits of participation, and to empower you to make an informed decision.

This research is part of a collaborative project between Michigan State University’s Quello Center in the Department of Media & Information, and Merit Network. Merit Network, founded by Michigan State University and the University of Michigan, is a non-profit member-governed organization providing Internet access to over 700 connections to Michigan’s community anchor, government, and non-profit institutions. You should feel free to ask the researchers any questions you may have.

Study Title: Citizen-Enabled Advances in Broadband Availability Data

1. PURPOSE OF RESEARCH        

The purpose of this study is to develop a better understanding of the availability and quality of broadband access in Michigan. Using an Internet-based application, the study collects technical data about your Internet connection and asks you to fill out a short survey.

2. WHAT YOU WILL DO

Participation in this study should take about 5 to 7 minutes. You will be asked to take brief survey which includes questions about Internet accessibility, pricing and places of use as well as your household size and income. By using our Internet-based application to answer a few survey questions, you will provide important information on your Internet access and allow the research team to test the speed and other technical features of your Internet connection. The data collected will include: Performance variables describing the state of connection during the test, such as average download and upload speed; latency and topology of the path between the user and server; and whether the application or protocol is manipulated or discriminated against in transit; information necessary to characterize performance data, including the Internet Protocol (IP) address and the date and time of the test and; and, descriptive information about the user’s environment to support analysis of measurement data. This may include the name and version of the web browser software, software application, or operating system used to conduct the test. In addition, the application will ask you to complete a brief survey about your Internet service, uses of the Internet, satisfaction with Internet access, access points, home ownership, number of people and children in the household and address.

3. POTENTIAL BENEFITS

We hope that our study will contribute to better broadband service in the future. You may or may not benefit personally from participating in this study. However, we hope that the citizens of Michigan at large, and possibly across the United States, will benefit from this study in the form of better and cheaper Internet access.

4. PRIVACY AND CONFIDENTIALITY

The study will respect your privacy and treat all information in a confidential way. No information will be stored in a publicly accessible way that would allow associating your history of tests or devices or reveal content of communications, such as emails or Internet searches. Data will be maintained and archived in a secure and password-protected repository at servers at Michigan State University and Merit Network, Ann Arbor.  Merit Network will host address and location data, however, this information will not be linked to personal data. It will be in a separate database and not accessible to the public, only to the researchers.

5. DATA POLICY

For technical network data, such as download speed, this study uses the M-Lab measurement platform that collects public data on Internet performance. The data is generated automatically when users conduct the voluntary test used in the web application for this study. All test data is retained indefinitely and published publicly to support research into trends in Internet performance. Please check the M-Lab data policy before you submit any data to the platform through the test.

6. YOUR RIGHTS TO PARTICIPATE, SAY NO, OR WITHDRAW

You have the right to say no to participate in the research. You can stop at any time after you started to answer the survey questions. There will be no consequences if you stop and you will not be criticized.

7. COSTS AND COMPENSATION FOR BEING IN THE STUDY

There are no foreseeable costs or compensations for participating.
